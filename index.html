OMINO

THE GAME

Overview

Roll the Omino towards a hole, assimilate cubes along the way to overcome obstacles.

Concept
Omino is an isometric puzzle game where the goal is to guide a cube-shaped character into a hole on a square grid. The cube, known as an Omino, moves around by rolling. Along the way, the player Omino will encounter passive Ominoes. The player Omino will attach itself to any passive Omino when rolled onto the tile adjacent to it (or dropped on top of it), forming a composite Omino called a Domino. Later puzzles introduce more than one passive Omino, requiring the formation of Triominoes and even Tetrominoes to be solved.

Inspirations
Omino’s main inspiration is an old browser game called Bloxorz. The mechanics and goal in Bloxorz are similar, involving rolling a block into a hole on a square grid. This block always consists of two cubes, and all levels take place on a single plane. Omino extends this concept by having the player start out as a single cube and be able to attach other cubes along the way, as well as introducing a third dimension where the player has to roll up ledges.

Target Audience
The primary target audience are casual players of all ages with interest in puzzle and brain games. Due to the simple interaction requiring only one finger from players as well as the possibility to pause the game flow at any time, our audience can hopefully fin pockets of time in daily life with a casual yet challenging mobile game.

Target Audience
The basic visual style of the game keeps the principle of simplicity and consistency. To be consistent with the game mechanics, the visual assets of both environment settings and characters mainly remains cube-shaped. The aesthetic design aims for a simple but endearing visual experience.

Gameplay & Mechanics

Reach the goal hole in as many levels as you can or let your creativity flow building your own levels.

Gameplay
The objective of the game is to help the cube reach the destination hole and successfully drop to the next level. The game demands problem solving skills, knowledge of game mechanics, spatial reasoning, computational and algorithmic thinking.

Progression
The levels in the game contain linearly increasing complexity and challenges. Players can unlock the next level and proceed ahead by solving the current puzzle.

Keep it loose - Don’t lose
To maintain the relaxing and casual characteristics of the game, this game does not have any frustrating losing conditions. The number of attempts per level are unlimited.

Level editor
The game also provides the possibility for players to customize their own levels. In the level editor mode, players can add any available block resources to the game scene and place them in the desired position to build their own unique puzzles.

Mechanics
During the movement towards the destination, the Omino will encounter various obstacles as well as some passive blocks that can be assimilated to form some more complex shapes, in order to overcome some of the aforementioned obstacles.

Movement
For the game control input, players only need to use a finger to roll the block by swiping to the desired directions on the screen of mobile devices. There are several cases of invalid movement. This normally happens if the next movement gets blocked by a wall or leads to falling from the edge. When the block detects an invalid position, the block will roll back to the last position and get a negative sound feedback.

Other Movement
The movement pattern can be influenced by the environment. Different properties of environment blocks can create some physical interactions with Omino. A typical example can be the movement on the ice, this will lead to a sliding motion of the Omino, instead of a roll, when there is a force applied.

Physics
The game simulates realistic physics in general. The falling of blocks also follows the gravity while the rolling of blocks is triggered by the force given by finger swiping from players.

Actions
Switches and Buttons - On some levels, there can be switches in different colors. When an Omino steps on a switch, it triggers the response from a certain environment setting such as a door or a bridge with the same color as the switch. Attaching and Detaching Blocks - Another unique mechanic in this game is the ability of Omino to assimilate other passive blocks and form complex shapes. At certain environment blocks, attached blocks can also be detached from the Omino.
